A Global Problem

Thousands of users face the same challenge every day: Smartwatches don't work on tattooed arms. Ink pigmentation blocks the sensor's optical reading.

Constant Locking

The watch asks for your password or PIN every few minutes because it thinks it's off your wrist.

The Failure of DIY Solutions

Many users try home remedies that end up damaging the equipment or frustrating the experience:

  • Resin and Epoxy Domes: Excessive thickness that moves the watch away from the wrist and prevents magnetic charging.
  • Latex Membranes: Opaque materials that distort the sensor light and give wrong heart rate readings.
  • Tapes and Bandages: They peel off with sweat, leave glue residue, and oxidize the sensor's metallic contacts.
