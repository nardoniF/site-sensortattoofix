About Us

Sensor Tattoo Fix was born from a real experience.

Sensor Tattoo Fix was not born in a distant lab or with secret aerospace engineering. It was born in Imirim, North Zone of São Paulo, at “Grandma's House” — where I was born and, years later, after Grandma Maria went to live in heaven, I chose to make my home.

Since childhood I have been crazy about tattoos; I could never see a badly made spider web pass by without getting hypnotized, and I bought every decal from the newsstand. Over time I became a great admirer and collector of tattoo art. I filled my body with ink and met many people in the tattoo world.

At 49, the need to track my workouts and take care of my health led me to buy the most up-to-date smartwatch on the market.

There it was — the imperfect mix: Tattoo + Smartwatch = Problem.

Frustration. With the equivalent of a motorcycle's worth on my wrist, the watch kept locking and asking for the passcode all the time. The turning point came on a walk with my uncle: the workout paused on its own every few meters.

Researching online, I found that thousands of people were going through the same hassle: dark tattoo pigment blocks the light beams of biometric sensors. The only solutions were hacks: improvised stickers that came off in the shower, generic films that distorted readings, and palliative fixes that risked damaging the device.

Selling the watch was an option — but erasing my tattoos? Never! Masters like Polaco, Mauro Landim, and André Rodrigues would never forgive me.

3N20 Soluções Tecnológicas has existed for more than 20 years, focused on software development — I never thought about putting effort into hardware solutions. With water-resistant adhesion and a lens capable of perfect light refraction through the ink: voilà! Sensor Tattoo Fix was born.

Sales grew through our official store and word of mouth: one sale, then 10, 40, 400.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why does my smartwatch ask for the passcode all the time?

When the optical sensor can't detect blood flow on your wrist, the watch assumes it was removed and asks for the passcode again — on Apple Watch this can happen every 10 seconds. On tattooed skin, ink blocks the sensor light; the watch activates PIN lock and thinks it left your arm. The Sensor Tattoo Fix lens restores the reading and stops constant lockouts.

Why won't my smartwatch measure heart rate on tattooed skin?

The sensor uses light to measure blood flow. Dense tattoos, scars or dark pigment block the return signal — heart rate shows zero, inaccurate readings, drops mid-workout or the watch stops monitoring entirely. The Sensor Tattoo Fix lens fixes the reading without disabling the sensor.

Why does my watch disconnect or lose wrist contact on its own?

Lost sensor signal makes the smartwatch think it left your arm: it unlocks, pauses workouts, stops health monitoring or asks for passcode again. If you have a tattoo where the watch sits, that's the most common cause — and there's a professional optical lens fix.

Why does my watch pause workouts and activities by itself on tattooed skin?

Because tattoo ink blocks the sensor's light, the watch cannot calculate your heart rate. The system interprets this signal loss as if you had stopped exercising or removed the device, pausing the activity automatically to save battery.

How does the Sensor Tattoo Fix lens correct the sensor?

Our lens technology was developed specifically to bypass the blocking caused by tattoo ink. It acts as a high-precision transmitter that allows the biometric sensor to read through tattooed skin, keeping all watch functions (payments, health and workouts) 100% operational.

Does it work on Apple Watch?

Yes. The Sensor Tattoo Fix lens works on Apple Watch and on virtually any smartwatch that uses an optical sensor (green or infrared light) on the back — Apple, Samsung, Garmin, Xiaomi and other brands. The kit includes common sensor diameters. Before and after demo on Apple Watch:

Does disabling pulse detection solve the problem?

No. Disabling pulse detection is the worst solution. It turns off essential features such as contactless payments (NFC), automatic security lock and continuous health monitoring. You effectively turn a cutting-edge smartwatch into a regular watch that only tells time.

How is the lens installed?

Installation is simple and quick. First, clean your watch sensor with the supplied isopropyl alcohol wipe. Peel the protective film from the very high-bond adhesive, align the lens with the center of the sensor and press gently for 30 seconds to ensure full adhesion.

Is there an installation manual?

Yes. Every kit includes a printed step-by-step guide. You can also view and print the full version here: Sensor Tattoo Fix installation manual (Portuguese).

Do I need to remove a protective film from the lens after sticking it?

No. The Sensor Tattoo Fix lens is ready to use. After sticking and pressing for 30 seconds, it is immediately ready. There is no film or layer on top of the lens to remove; it already has the ideal transparency and finish for sensor operation.

Does the lens get in the way of charging the watch?

No. It was developed with an extra-fine design — minimal thickness — so it does not interfere with fitting on the charging dock. You charge normally, including wireless charging, without removing the lens.

How long until the lens comes off? Does it peel off easily?

It is made to stay in place until you decide to remove it. It will not come off during workouts, over time, with sweat, in the sauna, in the pool, in the shower, when washing your hands or in water — you can swim and train intensely without fear of it peeling off. If you want to take it off to sell your watch or return it to its original state, simply pull it off by hand.

How long should I wait before getting it wet?

After installation, we recommend waiting 72 hours before exposing the watch to water, swimming or intense sweating. This is the ideal cure time for the very high-bond adhesive to reach maximum strength, ensuring a perfect and lasting seal.

Can I remove the lens later if I sell my watch?

Yes. Simply pull it off by hand — the lens is made to stay put day to day, but comes off whenever you want. Any adhesive residue can be cleaned with isopropyl alcohol, leaving the original sensor intact.
