Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why does my smartwatch stop monitoring heart rate on tattooed skin?

Your smartwatch sensor uses light to measure blood flow. Tattoo ink blocks or distorts that light, making the sensor lose the return signal. The watch then assumes it is off the wrist, causing training pauses or inaccurate readings.

How does the Sensor TattooFix lens correct the sensor?

Our lens is engineered to bypass the light-blocking effect of tattoo ink. It acts as a precision optical interface that allows the biometric sensor to read through tattooed skin, keeping payment, health tracking, and workout functions fully operational.

Does disabling pulse detection solve the problem?

No. Disabling pulse detection is the worst solution. It turns off essential features such as NFC payments, automatic security locking, and continuous health monitoring. You effectively turn a high-tech smartwatch into a regular timepiece.

How is the lens installed?

Installation is simple and fast. First, clean your watch sensor with the supplied alcohol wipe. Peel the protective film from the medical-grade VHB adhesive, align the lens over the sensor, and press firmly for 30 seconds for a secure bond.

Do I need to remove a protective film after sticking it?

No. The Sensor TattooFix lens is ready to use. After adhesion, it is immediately functional. There is no extra top film to remove; the lens is already calibrated for transparency and optimal sensor performance.

Will the lens come off in water or during a workout?

No. We use high-performance VHB adhesive that is extremely resistant to moisture, sweat, and submersion. You can swim, shower, and train hard without worrying about it peeling off.

How long should I wait before getting it wet?

After installation, wait 72 hours before exposing the watch to water, swimming, or intense sweating. This is the ideal cure time for the adhesive to reach full strength and maintain a perfect seal.

Can I remove the lens later if I sell my watch?

Yes. If you need to remove the lens later, gently pry it off with a plastic tool or fingernail. Any adhesive residue can be cleaned with alcohol, leaving the watch sensor intact.

Why does my watch pause workouts and physical activities by itself on tattooed skin?

Because tattoo ink blocks the sensor's light, the watch cannot calculate your heart rate. The system interprets this signal loss as if you had interrupted the exercise or removed the device, pausing the activity automatically to save battery.

What does it mean when the watch 'does not recognize the pulse' over tattooed skin?

Every smartwatch has a security lock that requires a continuous blood flow reading to know it is on the arm. When ink pigmentation prevents this reading, the watch activates PIN lock and disables smart features, because it thinks it was removed from the body.

Why does my smartwatch ask for the passcode all the time?

When the sensor cannot detect your pulse because of tattoo ink, the watch assumes it was removed and asks for the passcode again as a security measure. The Sensor TattooFix lens fixes this by restoring proper blood flow detection, preventing constant lockouts.
