Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why does my watch pause workouts and activities by itself on tattooed skin?

Because tattoo ink blocks the sensor's light, the watch cannot calculate your heart rate. The system interprets this signal loss as if you had stopped exercising or removed the device, pausing the activity automatically to save battery.

What does it mean when the watch 'does not recognize the pulse' over tattooed skin?

Every smartwatch has a security lock that requires continuous blood flow reading to know it is on your wrist. When ink pigmentation prevents this reading, the watch activates PIN lock and disables smart features because it thinks it was removed from your body.

Why does my smartwatch ask for the passcode all the time?

When the sensor cannot detect your pulse because of tattoo ink, the watch assumes it was removed and asks for the passcode again as a security measure. The Sensor TattooFix lens fixes this by restoring proper blood flow detection, preventing constant lockouts.

Why does my smartwatch stop monitoring heart rate on tattooed skin?

Your smartwatch sensor uses light to measure blood flow. Tattoo ink acts as an opaque barrier that absorbs or scatters that light, preventing the sensor from receiving the return signal. The watch then wrongly assumes it was removed from your wrist, causing automatic workout pauses or reading failures.

How does the Sensor TattooFix lens correct the sensor?

Our lens technology was developed specifically to bypass the blocking caused by tattoo ink. It acts as a high-precision transmitter that allows the biometric sensor to read through tattooed skin, keeping all watch functions (payments, health and workouts) 100% operational.

Does disabling pulse detection solve the problem?

No. Disabling pulse detection is the worst solution. It turns off essential features such as contactless payments (NFC), automatic security lock and continuous health monitoring. You effectively turn a cutting-edge smartwatch into a regular watch that only tells time.

How is the lens installed?

Installation is simple and quick. First, clean your watch sensor with the supplied isopropyl alcohol wipe. Peel the protective film from the very high-bond adhesive, align the lens with the center of the sensor and press gently for 30 seconds to ensure full adhesion.

Do I need to remove a protective film from the lens after sticking it?

No. The Sensor TattooFix lens is ready to use. After sticking and pressing for 30 seconds, it is immediately ready. There is no film or layer on top of the lens to remove; it already has the ideal transparency and finish for sensor operation.

Will the lens come off in water or during a workout?

No. We use a very high-bond adhesive that is extremely resistant to moisture, sweat and submersion. You can swim, shower and train intensely without fear of it peeling off.

How long should I wait before getting it wet?

After installation, we recommend waiting 72 hours before exposing the watch to water, swimming or intense sweating. This is the ideal cure time for the very high-bond adhesive to reach maximum strength, ensuring a perfect and lasting seal.

Can I remove the lens later if I sell my watch?

Yes. If you need to remove the lens later, carefully lift it with a thin plastic spatula or your fingernail. Any adhesive residue can be easily cleaned with a little isopropyl alcohol, leaving the watch's original sensor intact.
